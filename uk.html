CENSORSHIP IN MEDIA: GLOBAL PERSPECTIVES

FILM CENSORSHIP IN THE UNITED KINGDOM

While the United Kingdom is often seen as a free European country, there is still a sizable amount of censorship that occurs there. The primary organization responsible for film censorship is the British Board of Film Classification (BBFC).

The BBFC is the United Kingdom's primary rating agency, equivalent to the United States' Entertainment Software Rating Board (ESRB).

The BBFC does not have the final say on film ratings however. Local jurisdictions have the right to reveiw films and assign them a higher rating than they were given by the BBFC. One such example of this was the 1980 banning of Monty Python's Life of Brian in Glasgow theaters.

While many sources claim that the film was banned for being blasphemous, It was actually banned for its crude language and nudity.
